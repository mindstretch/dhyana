The complete brand foundation for Dhyana — what it believes, how it speaks, how it looks, and what it refuses to be.

01 · Foundation

What Dhyana Believes

The human nervous system is not broken. It is responding — accurately, intelligently — to the conditions it's been given. Stress is not a character flaw. Depletion is not weakness. Dysregulation is not failure. These are states. States can be changed.

Most people walk through their days without ever knowing where they actually are. Not emotionally, not physiologically. They are running on the nervous system equivalent of a car with no dashboard — vaguely aware something's off, never quite sure what to do about it.

Dhyana believes the most powerful thing a person can do — before the meditation, before the breathwork, before any protocol — is simply to know. To take three seconds and honestly place themselves:

"This is where I am right now."

That act of recognition is itself the intervention. Everything else flows from it.

02 · Foundation

What Dhyana Is

A nervous system intelligence. Not a meditation app, not a fitness tracker, not a wellness dashboard. Those things exist. Dhyana is something different: a system that reads where you are and tells you exactly what to do about it.

Phase 1 It asks you where you are.
Phase 2 It already knows.
Phase 3 It reaches you before you think to look.

What It Is Not

It is not a streak. There is no counter that resets if you miss a day. No badge for consistency. No gamification layer that turns self-knowledge into a score to protect.

It is not a diagnosis. It does not tell you what is wrong with you. It tells you where you are — and what, from here, might help.

It is not a meditation app with better branding. It gives you protocols matched to your actual state — the difference between a doctor who gives everyone the same prescription and one who actually reads your chart.

It is not clinical. It does not look like a medical device, a fitness tracker, or a health records portal. It looks and feels like something made for human beings who want to understand themselves.

03 · Foundation

The Emotional Promise

On opening

"Someone made this for me."

Not for "users." For the version of them that woke up at 3am unable to sleep, or sat at their desk at 2pm unable to remember what they were doing.

After check-in

"I know where I am now."

That small clarity — that moment of accurate self-location — is the product. Everything else is built on it.

04 · Voice

How Dhyana Speaks

Sparse

The minimum number of words that carry the full weight. Not minimalism for aesthetics — because the nervous system is already overloaded.

Warm

Speaks like a trusted companion who knows your nervous system — not a doctor reading from a chart, not a chatbot completing a task.

Direct

Does not hedge or over-explain. Says what it means. "You're running hot. Here's what will help."

Never Judgment

No good state. No bad state. There is only where you are. The language reflects this without exception.

06 · Voice

Tone by Surface

App UI — Check-in

"Where are you right now?"

The quietest version. Almost no words. What's there is precise and warm.

State reveal

"Restful Alertness. Calm and clear. Fully present. Ready."

Recognition, not diagnosis. A reflection, not a label.

Email

"You showed up running hot. Dhyana would know what to do with that."

One register warmer. Acknowledges the specific moment. Short. Never salesy.

Marketing / waitlist

"Dhyana reads your nervous system and tells you exactly what to do about it."

The most expressive version — still spare, but allowed to carry weight.

Push notifications (Phase 3)

"Your nervous system is activated right now. You have 8 minutes. Here's something that will help."

The most intimate surface. Feels like a message from someone who knows you.

Error states

"Something didn't work. Try again."

Warm, human. Zero frustration-inducing language. Never technical.

07 · Visual

The Three Words

Still

Nothing restless. Nothing anxious. Every element earns its place.

Warm

Not cold. Not clinical. A product made for human beings.

Alive

The orb breathes. The product has presence. It is not inert.

Every visual decision runs through these three words. If something feels restless — not Dhyana. If something feels cold — not Dhyana. If something feels inert — not Dhyana.

08 · Visual

Color

State colors are not decorative. They are meaning. Each maps to a precise position on the nervous system state space. They appear when a state is identified — never as UI chrome.

Restful Alertness

#E8A855

Warmth + clarity. Morning sun. The optimal state.

Regulated

#6FA89A

Steady. Neither warm nor cool. Balanced.

Activated

#C96B5A

Heat without alarm. Running hot.

Depleted

#5A6E8A

Heavy. Cool. Low energy, low light.

Dysregulated

#7A6880

Mixed signals. Uncertain.

Background

#0E1219

Deep navy-black. Not pure black (too harsh). Not dark grey (too neutral). This tone reads as night sky, not void.

09 · Visual

Typography

DM Serif Display

Dhyana

Wordmark, headlines, state reveals. Italic only. Serif warmth in a sans context — signals this is not a dashboard.

DM Sans 300

Where are you?

All UI, body copy, labels. Weight 300–400 only. Never bold — heaviness creates pressure, and pressure is the opposite of what Dhyana does.

Logo

Wordmark-first until a logomark is developed

11 · Application

What Dhyana Never Does

Brand is as much about what you refuse as what you claim.

Never guilts the user

No streak that breaks. No push notification that says you've been absent. No UI that implies you're doing it wrong.

Never overwhelms

One thing at a time. One protocol per session. One question at the core of check-in. One state at a time.

Never medicates with language

Does not tell people they are sick or broken. The word "anxiety" does not appear in the product. Neither does "disorder," "treatment," or "management."

Never gamifies the self

The person's nervous system is not a game. Badges, points, leaderboards, and social comparison have no place here.

Never compromises on the quiet

If something feels like it belongs in a productivity app, a fitness tracker, or a meditation app — it probably doesn't belong in Dhyana. The quiet is a feature. Protect it.

12 · Application

The Design Test

"Does this feel like taking a breath?"

✗ If it feels like filling out a form — start over.

✗ If it feels like opening a notification — start over.

✗ If it feels like a dashboard — start over.

✓ If it makes someone exhale slightly when they see it — you're there.
